01

The End of Monoculture

We no longer experience culture together

Hyper-personalized algorithmic feeds have eliminated shared cultural experiences. TikTok perfected the model—an algorithm so precise it feels psychic. The result: your feed is completely different from your customer's feed. Summer 2025 had no Barbenheimer, no Brat Summer. Just infinite fragmented micro-moments.

50%

fewer streams for #1 hits in 2024 vs 2017

Medium5

$144M

Visual Capitalist6

What it means for marketers

Your feed is not your customer's feed. Star power no longer guarantees reach. Understanding audience-specific interests through data (not assumptions) is critical.

02

The Great Questioning

Social media's reckoning is here

Jonathan Haidt's "The Anxious Generation" sold 2M+ copies—and it's driving policy. Australia banned social media for under-16s. The US Surgeon General called for warning labels. Florida restricted access for minors. Adults are holding up a mirror to their own habits too: usage is actually declining, and Gen Z is leading the digital detox movement.

The questioning IS translating to behavior change. Respect for people's attention is increasingly valued—because that attention is becoming more scarce.

03

Spectator Social

We're becoming watchers, not participants

The Wall Street Journal called it: people aren't posting anymore. Individuals are retreating from personal sharing while professional creators fill the vacuum. British Vogue asked "Is having a boyfriend embarrassing?"—revealing how personal updates now feel "cringe." We scroll, we watch, we don't post. Feeds are interest-based entertainment, not relationship-based connection.

What it means for marketers

Feeds are interest-based, not relationship-based. Brands and creators fill the gap. Content that invites spectating (not demanding participation) may resonate.

08

Creator Communities Off-Platform

Building influence outside the algorithm

People with online followings are building deeper connections with their audiences outside major social platforms. Substack has become the poster child—writers, journalists, and creators migrating to email where they own the relationship. With 5 million paid subscriptions and a $1.1B valuation, Substack proves the model works. These privileged spaces (paid newsletters, Discord servers, Patreon communities) are becoming powerful vectors of influence precisely because they're gated. The intimacy of the inbox beats the noise of the feed. Gatekeeping is back—and it's a feature, not a bug.

5M

paid Substack subscriptions (March 2025)

Substack / Hollywood Reporter17

$10B

Patreon18

$1.1B

Sacra / NY Times19

What it means for marketers

10,000 true fans > 100,000 followers. These gated spaces command higher trust and deeper engagement than open social feeds. Building owned communities provides insulation from algorithm changes—and creates powerful vectors for influence.

10K true fans > 100K followers
09

Mister Rogers Over Mr. Beast

Quality over quantity, calm over chaos

The pendulum is swinging from high-stimulation, shock-value content toward calm, genuine, memorable content. Retention over reach. Memorability over virality. One loyal viewer who watches 10 videos matters more than 10 people who scroll past.

✓ In

  • Mister Rogers-style content
  • Retention over 10 videos
  • Optimizing for memorability
  • Quality
  • "See my attention span is fine"

✗ Out

  • Mr. Beast-style content
  • Reach of one video
  • Optimizing for shock
  • Quantity
  • Brainrot

What it means for marketers

Focus on retention and memorability, not just reach. One loyal viewer who watches 10 videos > 10 people who scroll past.

Retention over reach
10

Offline Stunts & IRL Activations

"I was there" is cultural currency

In an AI-saturated world, "I was there, I saw that" has become cultural currency. Digital momentum should build toward real-world experiences. Offline whimsy as counterbalance to loud feeds. Ramp's "Funeral for the Penny." Josh Eckert the Leaf Cruncher.

✓ In

  • Offline stunts
  • "Momentum marketing" (digital → IRL)
  • Creators touching grass
  • Guerrilla social marketing

✗ Out

  • Online-only stunts
  • Pure digital campaigns
  • Algorithm-dependent reach

What it means for marketers

Digital should drive IRL. Turn digital momentum into real-world gatherings. "I was there" is cultural currency.
